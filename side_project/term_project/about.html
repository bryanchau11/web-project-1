1962

Sister Lisa Chau and Brother John Chau found a winery in Paris, France, producing 200,000+ gallons of wine at that time

1966

Sold 756,523 gallons of wine after 4 years of operation

1972

January 4th, Lisa and John appeared on the cover of the LE FIGARO for an article tilted "French Wine Age"

1973

A year later, Registered the Bordeauxien trademark.

1976

Established Bordeauxien, which is the biggest winery company at that time.

1980

Introduced Lisa&John wine coolers

1983

Opened first International office in Paris, released first vintage-dated wines.

1986

Purchased the Sommellerie de France in Marseille

1990

Wildfire burnt down Sommellerie de France, Bordeauxien remained.

1992

Introduced the wines nationally, Lisa passed away at age 89

1995

Introduced Bordeauxien to the States

2005

Ten years later, Received the International Standars Organization's ISO certification.

2006

Built 50+ stores in U.S, 60+ stores in EU

2012

Celebrated 50 years as family-owned winery.

2015

Bryan Chau became the winery CEO.

2016

John Chau passed away at age 90.

2020

Bordeauxien named #13 out of 100 "Best winery and Best place to work" in the nation.
